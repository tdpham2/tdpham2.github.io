Greetings! I am Thang Pham, a postdoctoral researcher at Argonne National Laboratory. My work centers on **agentic AI systems for scientific discovery** — designing multi-agent workflows and scalable agentic pipelines that run on leadership-class HPC systems and let scientists plan, reason about, and execute complex computational experiments through natural language.

I build AI systems that interface directly with the scientific stack: simulation codes (DFT, Monte Carlo, machine-learning interatomic potentials), databases and retrieval tools, HPC schedulers, and workflow engines. A central thread of my research is making autonomous, large-scale discovery workflows both practical and trustworthy — including frameworks for evaluating how well AI agents actually perform on real scientific tasks.

I earned my Ph.D. in Chemical and Biological Engineering at Northwestern University in the Snurr group, where I applied molecular simulation, machine learning, and optimization to materials discovery — spanning carbon dioxide capture, hydrogen storage, water harvesting, and photocatalysis. That background in computational materials science is the domain I now bring agentic AI to, with the goal of pioneering data-driven solutions that have a tangible impact on society.
